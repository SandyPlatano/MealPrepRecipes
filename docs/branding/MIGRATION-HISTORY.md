# Design System Migration History

This document tracks the evolution of the BWFD design system.

---

## Version 2.0 - Warm & Cozy (Current)
**Effective Date:** January 2026

### Overview
The current design system emphasizes warmth, approachability, and modern professionalism suitable for a family meal planning app.

### Key Characteristics
- **Color Palette:** Warm off-white background (`#FFFCF6`), lime accent (`#D9F99D`), near-black text (`#1A1A1A`)
- **Typography:** Plus Jakarta Sans (friendly, modern, professional)
- **Borders:** Subtle 1px borders (`border border-gray-200`)
- **Shadows:** Soft, subtle shadows (`shadow-sm`, `shadow-md`)
- **Border Radius:** `rounded-xl` for cards, `rounded-full` for buttons
- **Philosophy:** Warm & Cozy, Clean & Modular, Soft & Smooth

## Version 1.5 - Retro/Neo-Brutalist (Rejected)
**Exploration Date:** December 29, 2025
**Status:** Rejected, Archived

### Overview
A bold neo-brutalist design exploration with hard shadows and thick borders.

### Key Characteristics
- **Color Palette:** Warm beige background (`#FFFBEB`), golden yellow accent (`#FFD166`)
- **Typography:** Space Mono (headings), Work Sans (body)
- **Borders:** Heavy 2px black borders on all interactive elements
- **Shadows:** Hard drop shadows (`shadow-retro: 4px 4px 0px 0px #000`)
- **Philosophy:** Bold, playful, retro-inspired

### Rejection Reason
Too harsh and aggressive for a family-oriented meal planning app. The heavy borders and hard shadows created a jarring experience that didn't align with the warm, welcoming brand personality.

## Version 1.0 - Original Pale Lilac System
**Initial Launch:** 2025
**Status:** Superseded by Version 2.0

### Overview
The original brand system with a softer, more editorial feel.

### Key Characteristics
- **Color Palette:** Ivory background (`#FFFCF9`), pale lilac accent (`#DDC3E0`), chestnut illustrations (`#7D4A5A`)
- **Typography:** Manrope (sans-serif), Caveat (script), Cormorant Garamond (serif)
- **Philosophy:** Warm, editorial, high-contrast

### Migration to Version 2.0
The pale lilac system was replaced in favor of a more modern, professional design with better accessibility and stronger visual hierarchy.

## Design Philosophy Evolution

| Version | Philosophy | Visual Language | Target Feeling |
|---------|-----------|-----------------|----------------|
| 1.0 | Editorial, Personal | Soft pastels, script fonts | Intimate, handwritten |
| 1.5 (Rejected) | Neo-Brutalist | Hard shadows, bold borders | Playful, retro |
| 2.0 (Current) | Warm & Cozy | Subtle shadows, lime accents | Professional, inviting |

- Plus Jakarta Sans provides better readability than script-heavy designs
- Subtle borders and shadows work better for complex UIs

- Family-oriented apps need warmth, not aggression
- Heavy borders reduce usability in dense interfaces (meal planners, shopping lists)
