# The Journalist: Current State of Cooking & Shopping Apps (2024-2025)

**Research Date:** 2025-12-17
**Focus:** Market landscape, key players, latest features, and emerging trends

---

## Executive Summary

The cooking and shopping app market in 2024-2025 is characterized by AI integration, consolidation around major players, and increasing focus on end-to-end meal planning workflows. The market is maturing, with clear leaders in different segments and significant innovation happening at the intersection of cooking and shopping.

**Key Discovery:** AI is the defining trend of 2024-2025, with every major player racing to integrate recipe generation, personalization, and voice assistance. However, execution UX (cooking mode) remains the differentiator for serious users.

## Market Landscape 2024-2025

#### Yummly (Whirlpool)
**Position:** Largest recipe aggregator with smart appliance integration

**Key Features (2024):**
- 2M+ recipe database
- AI-powered personalization

## Key Trends 2024-2025

### Trend 1: AI Integration

**Current State:**
- Recipe generation from ingredients ("What can I make with chicken and broccoli?")
- Conversational recipe modification ("Make this dairy-free")
- Image recognition (photo fridge contents → recipe suggestions)
- Personalized recommendations based on natural language preferences

**Leaders:** Yummly, Tasty, emerging startups

**User Reception:** Mixed—excitement about capability, concerns about recipe authenticity and accuracy.

**Implication:** AI is table stakes for discovery but doesn't replace execution UX.

---

### Trend 2: Voice and Hands-Free

**Current State:**
- Alexa/Google Home skill integration mature
- In-app voice control improving
- "Hey [App]" wake word support emerging
- Voice navigation through recipe steps

**Leaders:** SideChef, Yummly, smart speaker integrations

**User Reception:** High demand; execution varies significantly.

**Implication:** Hands-free is essential for cooking mode; implementation quality matters.

---

### Trend 3: End-to-End Workflow

**Current State:**
- Meal planning → shopping list → grocery delivery → cooking guidance
- Single apps trying to own entire journey
- Integration partnerships vs. built-in features

**Leaders:** Mealime, Yummly, Whisk

**User Reception:** Strong preference for apps that "do it all" vs. juggling multiple apps.

**Implication:** Workflow completeness is competitive advantage.

---

### Trend 4: Smart Appliance Integration

**Current State:**
- Connected ovens receive cooking instructions
- Smart displays show recipes
- Temperature and timing sync
- Limited market penetration (10-15% of households)

**Leaders:** Yummly (Whirlpool), Whisk (Samsung)

**User Reception:** Enthusiastic among device owners; irrelevant to most users.

**Implication:** Nice-to-have, not essential; don't build strategy around it.

---

### Trend 5: Dietary and Health Focus

**Current State:**
- Robust dietary restriction filters (standard)
- Nutrition information (standard)
- Diet program integration (keto, paleo, etc.)
- Health app sync emerging

**Leaders:** Mealime, Yummly, MyFitnessPal integration partners

**User Reception:** Essential for dietary-restricted users; nice-to-have for general population.

**Implication:** Dietary filtering is table stakes; health integration is opportunity.

---

### Trend 6: Sustainability and Waste Reduction

**Current State:**
- "What's in my fridge" features emerging
- Expiration tracking limited
- Leftover recipe suggestions
- Carbon footprint tracking nascent

**Leaders:** Too Good To Go (different category), emerging features in meal planners

**User Reception:** Growing interest, especially among younger users.

**Implication:** Differentiation opportunity; aligns with user values.

## Current User Expectations (Benchmarks)

### Discovery & Browsing
- **Search:** Natural language understood ("quick weeknight chicken dinner")
- **Filtering:** Dietary, time, ingredients, cuisine, difficulty
- **Recommendations:** Personalized to preferences and history
- **Content:** Photos required; videos expected for complex techniques

### Meal Planning
- **Calendar view:** Drag-and-drop meal placement
- **Flexibility:** Easy changes, leftover nights, skip days
- **Household:** Shared visibility with family members
- **Suggestions:** "Fill my week" auto-planning option

### Shopping Lists
- **Generation:** Automatic from meal plan
- **Intelligence:** Combine duplicate ingredients
- **Organization:** By store aisle or category
- **Integration:** One-tap grocery delivery/pickup
- **Sharing:** Real-time sync with household

### Cooking Mode
- **Step-by-step:** One instruction at a time option
- **Timers:** Integrated, multiple simultaneous
- **Screen:** Auto-wake, high contrast
- **Voice:** Hands-free navigation
- **Progress:** Visual indicator of recipe progress

### Cross-Platform
- **Sync:** Seamless across phone, tablet, web
- **Offline:** Saved recipes available without internet
- **Export:** Ability to get data out

## Competitive Analysis Summary

### By Use Case

**"What should I cook tonight?"**
- Best: Yummly, Tasty (AI-powered suggestions)
- Runner-up: Mealime (curated simplicity)

**"Help me plan the week"**
- Best: Mealime, Plan to Eat (focused planning UX)
- Runner-up: Yummly (comprehensive but complex)

**"Guide me while cooking"**
- Best: SideChef (step-by-step voice guidance)
- Runner-up: Yummly (good cooking mode)

**"Manage my grocery list"**
- Best: AnyList (list-first design)
- Runner-up: Mealime (integrated planning)

**"Organize my recipe collection"**
- Best: Paprika (power user features)
- Runner-up: Copy Me That (simple capture)

## Market Gaps and Opportunities

### Gap 1: Cooking Mode Excellence
Most apps have adequate cooking modes; few are excellent. Opportunity for differentiation.

### Gap 2: Household Collaboration
Sharing exists but is typically basic. Deep household features are rare.

### Gap 3: Skill Development
Recipes assume skills; no apps do technique teaching well.

### Gap 4: Food Waste
Pantry tracking and expiration management are underserved.

### Gap 5: Budget Management
Few apps help with grocery budgeting and cost optimization.

### Gap 6: Cultural Diversity
International cuisines underrepresented in US-centric apps.
