# UX/UI Research Objective: MealPrepRecipes Application

## Core Research Questions

1. **What are the most impactful UX/UI patterns for meal planning applications?**
   - How do top meal planning apps structure their core workflows?
   - What interaction patterns reduce friction in recipe-to-meal-plan flows?

2. **How can we optimize the recipe discovery and management experience?**
   - What search/filter patterns work best for recipe databases?
   - How should recipe cards display information for quick scanning?

3. **What mobile-first patterns are essential for cooking applications?**
   - How should cooking mode be designed for kitchen use?
   - What gesture patterns work well with messy hands?

4. **How can we improve the shopping list and pantry experience?**
   - What UX patterns reduce friction in grocery shopping workflows?
   - How should pantry items be organized and surfaced?

5. **What social and community features drive engagement?**
   - How do successful recipe platforms handle user profiles?
   - What review/rating patterns encourage quality feedback?

6. **What accessibility and inclusive design patterns are critical?**
   - How do we serve users with dietary restrictions elegantly?
   - What visual hierarchy and contrast patterns improve usability?

## Application Context

- **Tech Stack**: Next.js 14, Shadcn/ui, Radix UI, Tailwind CSS, Supabase
- **Key Features**: Meal planning, recipes, shopping lists, pantry, nutrition, social
- **Target Users**: Home cooks doing weekly meal prep
- **Platforms**: Web-first, mobile-responsive PWA

## Success Criteria

- Identify 20+ actionable UX/UI improvements
- Document evidence-based patterns from successful apps
- Capture anti-patterns and common mistakes to avoid
- Provide implementation-ready recommendations
- Include accessibility considerations throughout

## Evidence Standards

- **Primary**: User research studies, A/B test results, analytics data
- **Secondary**: Expert reviews, design system documentation, case studies
- **Tertiary**: Blog posts, tutorials, community discussions

## Stakeholders & Perspectives

- **End Users**: Home cooks, meal preppers, families
- **Power Users**: Nutrition-conscious users, recipe creators
- **Accessibility Users**: Users with visual, motor, or cognitive needs
- **Mobile Users**: Kitchen use during cooking

## Biases to Guard Against

- Assuming desktop-first usage (cooking happens in kitchens with phones)
- Over-featuring (adding complexity instead of simplifying)
- Following trends without evidence
- Ignoring accessibility until "later"
- Copying competitors without understanding context
