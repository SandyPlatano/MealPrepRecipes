# The Journalist: Current State of Recipe Sharing (2024-2025)

## Key Players & Their Community Approaches

### Tier 1: Global Leaders

#### Cookpad
- **Users**: ~100 million monthly visitors globally, 76 countries, 23 languages
- **Japan**: 80-90% of Japanese women in 20s-30s are users
- **Model**: Pure user-generated content, no celebrities
- **Revenue Split**: 65% subscriptions, 25% advertising, 10% other services
- **Premium**: $2.50/month for advanced features
- **Community Philosophy**: Social incentives over monetary rewards

#### Samsung Food (Whisk)
- **Backing**: Samsung (acquired Whisk)
- **TikTok Partnership**: "See full recipe" button integration
- **Key Differentiator**: "Communities" feature - groups by diet (Keto, Vegan) or cooking style
- **Grocery Integration**: Direct connection to online retailers
- **Collaborative Features**: Shared collections, combined shopping lists
- **Pricing**: Free tier + $4.99/month or $35.99/year premium

#### AllRecipes
- **Legacy**: Founded 1997, pioneer of user-submitted recipes
- **Model**: User ratings, reviews, photo uploads
- **Challenge**: Competing with TikTok for discovery

### Tier 2: Focused Players

#### Paprika 3
- **Focus**: Personal collection management
- **Social Features**: Minimal - export-only sharing
- **Strength**: Cross-device sync, recipe clipping
- **Philosophy**: Solo ecosystem, not community

#### Pestle
- **Focus**: Personal cookbook curation
- **Social Features**: Deliberately minimal
- **Differentiator**: iCloud integration, clean UX
- **Philosophy**: "Perfecting your personal cookbook"

#### Mealime
- **Focus**: Meal planning and grocery lists
- **Target**: Busy families, health-conscious users
- **Community**: Limited, focus on utility

### Tier 3: Family/Household Focus

#### Family Daily
- **Key Feature**: Shared family meal planner with collaboration
- **Real-time Sync**: Shared shopping lists
- **Reminders**: "Cook when it's your turn" notifications
- **Target**: Households coordinating meals

#### Cozi
- **Broader**: Family scheduling + to-do + meal planning
- **Meal Planning**: Add-on to existing logistics
- **Sharing**: Multi-device family access

#### Maple
- **Features**: Shared calendar, grocery integration, in-app chat
- **Differentiator**: Real-time family discussion for meal preferences

### Tier 4: AI-First Newcomers

#### Ollie (2025)
- **Pitch**: AI-powered meal planning assistant
- **Learning**: Adapts to family tastes over time
- **Target**: "Real-life households, not perfect Pinterest boards"

#### Kitchee
- **Focus**: AI recipe generation and personalization
- **Features**: Dietary customization, ingredient-based suggestions

## Recent Developments (2024-2025)

### Shutdowns & Exits
- **Yummly** (April 2024): Whirlpool lays off entire 75-person team
- **Yummly** (Dec 2024): Website and app officially shut down
- **Industry Signal**: Appliance brands deprioritizing human-curated content for AI

### New Integrations
- **Pinterest + Walmart** (Dec 2025): "Shop Ingredients" button on recipe Pins
- **TikTok + Whisk**: Recipe integration within TikTok platform

### AI Disruption
- Generative AI for recipe creation emerging
- Personalization engines using ML for recommendations
- Concerns: AI-generated recipes vs. tested human recipes

## TikTok as Dominant Force

### By the Numbers
- #TikTokFood: 4.6 million posts
- #FoodTok: 4.5 million posts
- Single viral video can get 42 million views (Logan's Cucumber Salad)
- Tini's Mac and Cheese: 132 million views, 13 million likes

### 2024 Viral Trends

2. Dubai Chocolate Bar (140K posts with #dubaichocolate)

### Cultural Impact
- "Home cooks are now as likely to inspire food trends as celebrity chefs"
- "Recipes can go from unknown to viral overnight"
- Short-form video dominates discovery
- Recipe apps must integrate or compete with TikTok

## Market Dynamics

### Growth Trends
- Recipe app market growing with meal planning features
- AI personalization becoming table stakes
- Grocery integration as key differentiator
- Family/household features gaining importance

### Revenue Models in Use
- **Freemium**: Free tier + premium ($5-10/month typical)
- **Advertising**: Display ads, sponsored recipes
- **Affiliate/Commerce**: Grocery partnerships, commission on purchases
- **Subscriptions**: 57% of apps now use subscription model
- **Premium margins**: 30-50% for subscription apps

### Benchmark Statistics
- Top quartile DAU/MAU: >35%
- 90th percentile DAU/MAU: ~50%
- Free-to-paid conversion: 1-10% typical
- App subscription revenues (2024): $66.8 billion globally

## What's Working NOW

1. **Community-curated content** (Cookpad model)
2. **Diet/lifestyle-specific groups** (Samsung Food Communities)
3. **Grocery integration** (Pinterest + Walmart)
4. **Short-form video** (TikTok dominance)
5. **Family coordination** (Family Daily, Maple)
6. **AI personalization** (Ollie, emerging players)

## What's Declining

1. **Celebrity chef-driven platforms**
2. **Pure editorial content** (Yummly closure)
3. **Recipe apps without mobile-first design**
4. **Standalone recipe managers without integration**
5. **Paid-only apps** (freemium dominates)
